I've wanted to move away from using gmail for a long time (mostly because privacy, but other reasons as well) and buying a domain (stemen.email in my case) was the first step. This serves two purposes

  • you get to choose your exact email
  • you can change providers when you decide their privacy policy is no longer up to your standards
I first tried self hosting email, but found the difficulty to be slightly more than I wanted to do at the time. I may try again in the future, because it does have lots of pros. It is a slight privacy sacrifice, but communicating with other sometimes take priority over complete privacy, and most people I email probably use gmail anyway.

After a moderate amount of research on email hosting providers I landed on fastmail as a company that takes privacy seriously, the modernization of email seriously, and user experience seriously.
